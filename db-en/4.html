European Policy of China

Geopolitically speaking, the European policy of China may be basically summarized as follows: glue and help; divide and rule.

China does not intend to disintegrate EU since it wants a powerful peer to balance US and Russia, but neither does it want to see a united Europe as powerful as China itself is. The best place is where everyone else is an enemy of each other but they are all friends of you. The demand for dividing exists not only inside Europe, but also among US, Europe, and Russia. If Europe is too weak, China helps to glue it up; if Europe is too strong, China makes interference in the regional affairs and wait for European countries to dislike each other.

In the upcoming decades, China will be increasingly interested in establishing a paramilitary presence in Europe to safeguard its Maritime Silk Road security as part of its Euro-Indian strategy (a funny name indeed), regardless of whether it may admit or not.

Chinese people are united. Chinese people are cooperative with epidemic control measures which are found infeasible, even daydreaming, in Europe. Now, who is the real sick man of humanity?
