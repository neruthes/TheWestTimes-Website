Red Regime amid the Coronavirus Outbreak

Early last December, a cluster of pneumonia cases was reported in China. Little did the authorities know that it was not the common cold or influenza, but a whole new disease. Later that month, several whistleblower doctors warned their colleagues and friends in online chat groups that the cases might be related to SARS, an epidemic disease that swept the globe in 2003. The authorities did not deem the warnings from the doctors acceptable, slamming them as "spreading misinformation".

"You are hereby officially warned for posting false information on the internet. Such behavior constitutes a serious disturbance of social order and a violation of the law," a document from the police to one whistleblower declared.

Within two weeks, "misinformation" about a SARS-related disease outbreak became widespread. Sources indicated that the disease was capable of spreading from person to person, which health authorities in Wuhan denied. The government officials downplayed the severity of the outbreak. When a Chinese New Year feast involving thousands of people was held in Wuhan, the authorities turned a blind eye to it.

That was when Death came creeping in. On January 9, 2020, the first death in China was confirmed.
